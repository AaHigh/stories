A Cinematic Essay

What if the glyphs were real?

This is not a film you watch. It is a film you enter.

"Use the Source, Luke" is a truth-seeking documentary treatment that takes seriously a provocative hypothesis about the DMT experience: if the phenomenon is objective rather than purely subjective hallucination, then the intricate glyphs and visible language structures repeatedly reported could, in principle, be recorded and decoded.

Yet the public narratives — from Terence McKenna’s machine elves to modern Reddit reports and Strassman’s clinical studies — have never gone to that depth. They remain vivid, consistent in structure, and often profoundly meaningful… but ultimately anecdotal.

THE CORE CLAIM

If it’s objective,
then the glyph can be read.

The hypothesis is simple and falsifiable in principle:

  • Recurring glyph patterns should appear across independent experiencers above chance.
  • A visible syntax or grammar should be detectable through structured logging and computational analysis.
  • Information should be extractable — not just awe or personal insight, but compressible, transmissible, or verifiable content.
Why this matters

Most psychedelic research stops at phenomenology or therapeutic outcomes. This hypothesis asks for something harder: operational decoding.

Current state: Thousands of reports exist. Structured, large-scale glyph capture and inter-subject validation? Almost none.

The Documentary

Use the Source, Luke

An 18-minute conceptual web documentary. Truth-seeking. No advocacy. No sensationalism.

01 — THE MEMBRANE
0:00 – 2:30
Breakthrough. The world folds. For many who have crossed this threshold — whether in controlled research or elsewhere — the first consistent report is not chaos, but structure. Geometric. Linguistic. Alive.

Rick Strassman’s volunteers described “alphabet-like figures,” “a fantasy alphabet, a cross between runes and Russian or Arabic writing.” It felt like data. Not random noise.

Terence McKenna spoke of entities that did not merely speak — they offered visible language. Objects that were simultaneously gifts, machines, and syntax. The message was not heard. It was beheld.
02 — THE VISIBLE LANGUAGE
2:30 – 5:30
Classic accounts describe entities “made out of” the visible language itself. Meaning arrives multi-modally: visual, auditory, tactile, emotional — all at once. The chrysanthemum pattern is the elves is the message.

If this is objective — an external informational ecology rather than brain-generated spectacle — then the glyphs constitute a code. And codes, by definition, can be recorded, transcribed, and analyzed.
03 — THE HYPOTHESIS
5:30 – 9:00
Here is the testable claim: If the domain is real and shared, independent observers should converge on stable glyph inventories. Recurring symbols should appear above chance. Syntactic rules should emerge. Semantic payloads — if present — should yield extractable information.

Attempts exist at the edges. Laser diffraction experiments produce reports of consistent “code-like” flowing symbols. Some researchers have begun cataloguing them. Large-scale analyses show striking structural consistency.

But extraction at the level the hypothesis demands? That step is still missing.
04 — THE WALLS
9:00 – 13:30
Why hasn’t this been done?

Ineffability. The experience routinely exceeds sober descriptive capacity.
Temporal fragility. Peak effects last minutes. Memory and motor control collapse exactly when the glyphs are clearest.
Methodological gap. No standardized protocol for immediate glyph capture at scale.
Explanatory parsimony. Most researchers attribute everything to 5-HT2A agonism and elevated brain entropy — internally generated novelty.

Most critically: no documented case has delivered extractable, independently verifiable information from the glyphs. That is the anecdotal boundary.
05 — WHAT WOULD IT TAKE?
13:30 – END
To move beyond anecdote would require pre- and immediate post-experience structured logging, large-N multi-site studies, computational glyph analysis, and explicit tests for information content.

Some of this work is beginning. But it remains early.

The DMT reports are among the most consistent and extraordinary in the literature of altered states. They invite the question: Is this a source — an objective informational layer — or the most elaborate mirror the human mind can generate?

Until the glyphs are captured, catalogued, and stress-tested for extractable content at scale, the deeper claim stays unproven. The narratives remain powerful testimony, not yet decoded signal.

Use the source, Luke. But bring better recording equipment.
