For years, California’s CARE program has been sold as a lifeline — a 30-35%+ discount on electricity for qualifying low-income households. In practice, for many customers, it has become a bureaucratic nightmare.

Documented Cases

Live Oak / Sacramento Area (ABC10 “10 On Your Side,” early 2025)
A customer (“Miche”) qualified for CARE but never received the discount. She was told repeatedly it was “a problem with our system.” Months passed with full-rate billing. Only after ABC10 intervened did PG&E apply the CARE discount retroactively and issue a credit, acknowledging the delay was “an error on our side.”

SDG&E Mass Disenrollment (CPUC Resolution UEB-015, approved Dec 2024)
Due to problems with a new customer information system, San Diego Gas & Electric wrongly disenrolled 38,666 customers from CARE, FERA, and Medical Baseline programs. The CPUC found violations and imposed a $2.625 million penalty paid by shareholders. All affected customers were eventually reinstated and reimbursed.

These are not isolated incidents. They reflect a pattern tied to software limitations, post-enrollment verification failures, and complications with complex properties (multiple units, split lots, ADUs).

The Financial Impact

When eligible customers lose their CARE discount, they pay full rates — often $0.40–$0.50+/kWh. Repeated cycles easily turn into thousands of dollars in overcharges. Many only get resolution after external pressure (media, formal complaints, or legal escalation).

What You Can Do

  • Check every bill for the explicit “CARE Discount” line item.
  • Keep detailed records of all applications and communications.
  • For properties with multiple addresses or units, expect extra friction.
  • Retroactive credits are possible but often require persistence.

These failures are documented in media reports and CPUC records. The pattern is real.
